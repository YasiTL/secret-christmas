On the second day of Christmas, my true love gave to me… two prime numbers.

Santa loves math! Too bad he sucks at it. That’s why he has you. He’s working with prime numbers. He can’t tell you why he needs this info, but it seems pretty important so I guess you should do it (Santa likes to act as if everything is confidential). Anyway, he wants to find the first Mersenne Prime that exceeds 1000. Seems simple enough. But of course, Santa only wants the first Mersenne Prime whose exponent p is also prime. Go figure. Recall that a Mersenne Prime has the form n = 2^p - 1 where p is a positive integer. Also keep in mind that the formula above does not necessarily generate a prime number--it’s just a good way to find them.

Given this, what is the sum of the Mersenne Prime and the prime exponent p?
